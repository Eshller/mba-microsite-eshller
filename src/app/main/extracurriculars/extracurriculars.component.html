Visa Requirements
As a foreign student intending to study in the US you need to acquire a valid student Visa. For academic purposes, one needs to apply for an F1 Student Visa. Apart from allowing you to pursue your MBA, an F1 visa holder is allowed to stay back for up to one year after completing their graduate studies. Detailed instructions on how to apply can be found here. If you wish to stay back after the 1-year buffer period you will need to apply for a work visa.
A few tips:
  • You will need to pay a fee to book an interview slot before the application fee. You can pay it through any bank using NEFT.
  • Always complete Form DS-160 before the student visa interview, after which you should pay the application fee. Be sure to carry a printout!
  • The I-20 form will be issued by the university. Pay the fee online via the U.S. Immigration and Customs website.
  • Book your interview appointment through the VFS website. Make sure you have the visa fee receipt and DS-160 barcode number handy.

Essential documents to carry for the interview:
  • A valid passport and previously issued passports
  • Passport size photograph with a white background (50mm x 50mm)
  • DS-160 confirmation page with barcode
  • Visa application fee receipts
  • VFS appointment letter
  • SEVIS receipt and I-120 form

Visa not approved or rejected
In many cases, visas might not be approved and applicants have to wait until it is approved by the consulate. If this happens to you, you need not lose hope! Contact the consulate and apply again by paying the processing fees (again) and schedule your appointment.

Visa and Immigration Challenges

General Tips for Visa Process:
  • For example, let’s say your sixth year of H-1B status will end on September 1, 2021. As long as a U.S. employer filed a PERM or I-140 on your behalf before September 1, 2020, and the application is still pending on September 1, 2021, you are eligible to extend your H-1B status for one year.
  • Importantly, there is no limit to how many times an H-1B worker can use these AC21 provisions to extend a visa. Due to the major backlog in priority dates for some categories, you could potentially be in H-1B status for ten, 15, or 20 years while waiting for your priority date to become current
  • Also, be sure to talk with your DSO or foreign student advisor before you begin your internship hunt to fully understand these visas. (while in School. Post school, there are numerous provisions provided by the HR of the company you are working with to help you with your VISA complications)
  • Most students choose to use their CPT internships during their enrollment, keeping their OPT internships for after graduation as it is the only legal way they can stay in the country and look for a job if they do want to remain in the country permanently.
  • In brief, the PERM process requires the petitioning employer to conduct a series of recruitment activities to test the labor market before filing the application. If sufficient able, qualified, and willing applicants (U.S. citizen or Permanent Resident) are not found for a position through the recruitment process, the employer can submit a PERM labor certification application.

Arrival in the U.S.A
Make sure you have all the valid documents along with you - carry different copies of the documents with you (including your passport). If you do not have the appropriate documentation you will be sent back by the U.S. Department of Homeland Security.
