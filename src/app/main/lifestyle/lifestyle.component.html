Application Process

When to start?
There are three stages of the admissions process: pre-application, application and post-application.
“If you are considering a US business school, you should start thinking about how to best present your profile and candidacy a couple of years before application. This would allow you to identify strengths and major gaps in your profile ( employer brand, recommenders, volunteer work, GPA), and give you ample time to strengthen or course correct.”
- Parul Purwar, Kellogg School of Management

The  pre-application process  is when you research and decide which MBA program is best for you.
  • Consult USA MBA rankings
  • Review programs
  • Attend open house events and visit campuses, if possible

Best ways to study for GMAT
The GMAT features mathematics, grammar, and other subjects you likely studied many years ago. To increase your test performance for the exam, you could take a GMAT prep course (like JEE coaching) from test prep services like Jamboree, Manhattan, Veritas and Kaplan, if required. Do keep in mind that GMAT scores are valid for 5 years. GMAT is a computerized adaptive test, i.e. if you answer the current question correctly, the next one will be one level difficult and one level easier if you answer it incorrectly. The more you solve difficult questions correctly, the better your GMAT score will be. Since it is an adaptive test, you cannot skip any questions, and there is no negative marking in GMAT.

What are the four sections of the GMAT?
1. Analytical Writing Assessment: Consists of a 30-minute writing task basis analysis of an argument. It is important to be able to analyze the reasoning behind a given argument and critique it.
2. Integrated Reasoning: Designed to measure a test taker's ability to evaluate data presented in multiple formats from different sources.
3. Quantitative: Seeks to measure the ability to reason quantitatively, solve quantitative problems, interpret graphic data, and analyze and use the information given in a problem. Questions require knowledge of algebraic, geometric, and arithmetic concepts.
4. Verbal: Includes reading comprehension(RC), critical reasoning(CR), and sentence correction(SC). Each question type has five options to select from.

Section Duration in minutes Number of questions
Analytical writing assessment 30 1 essay
Integrated reasoning 30 12
Quantitative* 62 31
Verbal* 65 36
*Quantitative and Verbal are the only sections considered for marking.

How to prepare for the GMAT strategically?
Step 1:  Take a practice test to know your current stance, NOT from the Graduate Management Admissions Council (GMAC) website. And prepare a study plan accordingly.
Step 2: Find the average GMAT scores for the schools you wish to apply to and set a goal to score at or above the average. The image shows a formula for getting high scores of around 760. Only Verbal and Quant are considered for GMAT score.

Step 3: Strategize, plan, study & practice: It takes 3 months of 15-20 hours per week of dedicated studies to prepare well. One of the hardest parts of studying for the GMAT is managing time. Figure out a schedule and stick to it. The most  recommended source  of studying is the  Official Guide(OG)  published by GMAC.
A sample schedule of self-study could consist of concept & basic level questions every weekday from 5-8 PM, alternate days of Verbal and Quant and solving advance level question banks on weekends from the topics of the week.
“Finding a productive space to study can also be challenging. When I was studying for the GMAT, I had three roommates and knew my productivity when I got home would be limited. I decided to join a local coworking space so that I had a quiet, designated place to study at night and on the weekends. Your local coffee shop or library are also great places to study.”
Review log of errors: Top scorers of GMAT maintain a note of their weak and strong areas and find a pattern of mistakes. Below is one such way of monitoring your progress.
  • Keep track of any mistakes
  • Over time, look for patterns in your mistakes
Step 4: Regroup: Don’t be afraid to give the GMAT multiple shots. Most of the candidates take the GMAT two or three times before they achieve a score they are happy with. Admissions committees actually look positively on multiple attempts because it shows you have fortitude and are really committed to getting an MBA.

The biggest challenge was managing work and MBA applications simultaneously. It is a long process and can definitely be draining. GMAT prep is just the first step, and I gave my GMAT very early in the process to allow myself a breather.
- Parul Purwar, Kellogg

Why does GMAT matter?
  • A great GMAT (750+) offsets a bad CPI/GPA
  • Apart from getting you into a great school & program, it tremendously helps in getting scholarships (GMAT score based scholarships)
  • Apart from MBA, a great GMAT score in your CV helps when it comes to other job opportunities
Though due to COVID-19, many US schools have relaxed GMAT scores cut off or made it optional to submit. It is still in your favour to give the exam because of the other factors mentioned above.

Follow these best practices from test-takers who have taken the GMAT exam:
  1. Start your GMAT exam process at least six months before your test results are due. Most test-takers report a minimum eight-week study timeline if they are somewhat familiar with the underlying GMAT exam content. But you are the best judge of how much time you need to prepare.
  2. Review and study one section of the test at a time. Target your preparation for a specific section of the exam. Practice diagnostic and adaptive prep for quantitative reasoning.
  3. Review basic math skills.
  4. Practice pacing because time management is critical to completing the GMAT exam.
  5. Review the types of questions in the Verbal and Quantitative Reasoning sections.
  6. Use official GMAT Practice Tests to help you become familiar with the format used and questions asked in the actual exam.
  7. Practice with real GMAT questions.
Popular GMAT test series
  • Must do: GMAC official free tests-1 and 2; GMAC paid tests - 5 & 6; save tests 3 & 4 for later if one needs to give GMAT again
  • Advised: Manhattan prep series and Veritas prep tests
  • Should ideally give 6-10 practice mocks before the final exam
Essay writing preparation
Don’t fixate on getting a perfect essay in the first go. Let your essays go through many iterations
- Nidhi Gupta, Ross School of Business

Your business school essays are about bringing your story to life and providing context to your application by connecting the dots. You’ll want to capture an admissions reviewer’s attention with your story and authenticity, persuading them to learn more by inviting you to interview. This is also a great place where you can explain something negative like a bad GPA or GMAT score or any negative point in your profile. To get there, your essays should convey a strong sense of who you are as a person and what makes you tick, up and beyond what the admissions team will glean from your academic record and work history.
The topics of essays are released in summer with round 1, in the months of June or July. They are fairly open-ended, an example being the Harvard MBA Essay for 2022-2023:
“As we review your application, what more would you like us to know as we consider your candidacy for the Harvard Business School MBA program?” (no word limit)
Below are some strategies for a successful set-up, along with ideas to consider for crafting your stand-out MBA essay:

How to begin: Consider the big picture as you sit down to write. The ability to be both genuine and reflective about fundamental questions – such as motivations, goals, strengths and career aspirations – will form the basis for crafting a powerful narrative about who you are and where you’re going.
Brainstorm:What factors have driven your personal life and professional career?

Reflect:What key messages do you want to convey? What are the three most important takeaways you want the reader to know?
Frame:Start with a bullet points’ version and show a logical flow.

You should try to answer the question about what is unique in your profile that the admissions committee would appreciate. Once you do, build on top of it - whether it is via your work experience or via your extracurriculars. If your profile is unique and non-replicable, then you have the best chance of getting in.
- Kriti, Harvard Business School

Step back before going forward:Think about memorable experiences and why they brought you great happiness or a particularly rewarding challenge. Consider what you want a business school to take away from your essay before recounting a story.
A few ideas to consider:
  • What are your major accomplishments and why do you consider them so?
  • Does any attribute, quality or skill distinguish you from everyone else? How did you develop it?
  • What was the most difficult time in your life, and why? How did your perspective on life change because of that?
  • Have you ever struggled greatly for something and succeeded? What made you successful?
  • Have you ever struggled thus and failed? How did you respond?
  • What are your most important extracurricular or community activities? What made you join and continue these activities and why are you passionate about them?
Reverse the mirror: Now, think about it from the program’s perspective: they want students who love the school! Understand what makes it special and explain why it’s a great fit. They also want to understand how your presence and participation will enhance the overall experience—for others as well as for yourself. Show that you understand what the school cares about and that its values are aligned with your own.
  • What are your dreams for the future? When you look back on your life in 30 years, what would it take for you to consider your life successful?
  • How does this particular program fit into your future plans?
  • And, most importantly, why the MBA?
Get inspired: Avoid working in isolation:   Especially if you’re feeling stuck, seek some inspiration or entertainment in other writing.
  • Read sample admission essays.
  • Consider working your childhood experiences into your essay.
  • Consider your role models.
  • Ask for feedback from supervisors, colleagues, friends, professors and relatives.
  • Reflect upon self-assessment tools you might have taken in the past, such as (site) CliftonStrengths, and how your unique set of talents and traits can be highlighted.

Once you’re sufficiently prepared and inspired, start to play with possibilities. Dare to have a little fun here – humans respond to enthusiasm and your voice conveys your personality. Remember, there is no right story other than your own. And you are the person best poised to tell that story.

Think of a business school class as an auditorium with empty seats, each reserved for the profile of the candidate. If you match the profile, you get the admit. If someone matches the profile better than you, they get it.
- Swapnil Chichani, Kellogg

Different schools stress on different things, so there is not one recipe that can work for all schools.

Personal Interview Preparation
Most of the business schools don't follow the same style when it comes to Interviews. The personal interview is definitely one of the most important steps in the process because there is a chance you might stand out in your interview even if your application isn’t that compelling. Not to mention that if you receive an MBA interview invitation from a top business school, it’s a clear sign that the school believes in your potential and are considering you among the top candidates.
Different business schools are known to have a specific style and ask specific questions in their respective interviews, and this applies to most of the top 15 business schools in the USA. One can expect to experience different types of interviews for different programmes and that’s why you should familiarize yourself with each kind of interview format of your programme + target B-Schools and prepare accordingly. MBA consultants
The community has varied opinions about the impact MBA consultants have on a candidate’s chances to the top business schools. In this section we comprehensively discuss the merits and demerits of hiring consultants for your MBA application.
Due to our education system, we are not the most comfortable writing long essays that are catchy, eye grabbing and thoughtful. Getting help from some of the top consultants can make this a lot easier.
- Kriti Gupta, Harvard Business School

1) Who are MBA consultants and what do they do?
Most consultants are either former members of a business school's admissions office or are MBA graduates themselves and therefore have experience of what business schools look for in applicants and what it's like to study at particular schools. Most of the candidates seek the services of an MBA consultant around six months before the application deadline. There are various packages available depending on the needs of the candidate. More often than not, these packages are expensive and out of reach of most of the candidates with a three school package costing more than 5000 dollars quite easily.

2) Should you hire them?
Merits:
Candidates with communication challenges, especially the ones who cannot express themselves well in English, find the consultancy path much smoother. Although the services offered are mostly tailored to the candidate, some of what the consultants do include
  • Suggesting the candidates a proper way to frame the SoP, essay, resume
  • Shortlisting business schools and discussing their profile-school fit
  • Help them in proofreading and framing documents
  • Conducting mock interviews and instructing candidates how to present themselves in front of the business school’s admission team
A candidate may also opt for complete B-school wise packages from school selection to admission, if he or she needs more guidance or assistance during the process.
Demerits:
Apart from the obvious financial commitment, a common complaint by the candidates is that their consultant did not devote enough time to their application. To save themselves from this situation, one must ask how many clients the consultant works with for one cycle. This will give the candidate a clear picture.
