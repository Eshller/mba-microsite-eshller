Deferred MBA in the USA
There are several opportunities to apply for your MBA in your final year of college without accumulating work experience. A deferred MBA is one such option. Deferred MBA programs allow students in full-time undergraduate or master’s programs to secure a seat in a future class. What is it and is it right for you?
Why apply to deferred MBA programs?
The incentives that drive college and pre-experience graduate students to apply are tremendous:
  • Enhanced career flexibility: Many MBA aspirants fear that the time and investment to pursue an MBA may not be worth it. However, applying for a deferred MBA program enables you to plan your future in a more relaxed manner. If you wish to change your career path, you can always withdraw your application. It gives you 2-3 years right after graduate school to explore new interests and try out new things without worrying much about how it will affect your career.
  • Building a wider network:Upon getting accepted to a deferred MBA program, you gain immediate access to the school’s network of students, alumni and faculty. This provides you with professional opportunities before you even go to a business school.
DOWNSIDES TO CONSIDER Some might say that once you commit to a deferred MBA program, you lose out on consideration of other career options. If you are uncertain about your choices, enrolling in a deferred MBA program might not be the best decision. On the other hand, you could have more flexibility to pursue other careers as you know you have an MBA seat already saved.
Without clarity about your specialization, employers looking for pre-MBA experience in the same field may hesitate to hire you. You might also miss the chance to explore other fields after your MBA program completion.
When you think of an MBA, always think of it as a wild card. You can do it only once in your life, and it can be the best way to switch careers, industry or even geography.
- Kriti Gupta, Harvard Business School



I feel that it is useful to gain some work experience before applying for business school so that you really know why you are doing it. With the number of options and career paths available to individuals ranging from going for a Technical Masters to PhD to Business degree to joint Business + Engineering degree or joint Business+Law or Business+Policy degrees, it would be really helpful to choose the program and university that will be a good fit for you personally and in alignment with your future goals. Having a deferred admit to some of these good business schools may have the latent and subconscious effect of biasing you towards a certain type of career. I’d prefer that we all go into our careers post IITB unbiased and with an open mind and allow our experiences to shape our perspectives.
- Rahul Nechlani, Sloan

Beyond crafting a meaningful MBA journey, my hunch is that deferral program candidates will fare equally well in highly structured recruiting processes such as consulting or banking but might face challenges in more unstructured self-led job search processes since companies expect MBA candidates to have roughly 5 years of work experience and at least 3 years of work experience before their MBAs. However, that’s just my hunch. Reality could be different.
